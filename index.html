About

The Republic of Maldives is an archipelago consisting of a collection of atolls (a coral island surrounding a lagoon) in the Indian Ocean. The Maldives is located south-southwest of India, about 700 km south-west of Sri Lanka. This country has 26 atolls which are divided into 20 administrative atolls and 1 city.

The Maldives is a country with the smallest population and area in the Asian region and is 100 percent Muslim. The average height of the land surface in the Maldives is only 1.5 meters above sea level, making it the country with the lowest level in the world. The highest peak of the Maldives only reaches 2.3 meters above sea level, so it is also known as the country with the lowest highest peak in the world. The state of the Maldives economy depends on two main sectors: tourism and fishing. This country is well known for its many beautiful beaches and interesting underwater scenery, with around 700,000 tourists visiting the islands every year. Catching and processing fish makes the Maldives one of the fish exporters to several Asian and European countries.
